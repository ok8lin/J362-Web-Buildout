A Look Into Griffy's Art Supply

Griffy's is a locally-owned art store that has been open for a little more than a year. During that time, it has become a convenient resource for Bloomington artists and Indiana University students.

By Kaitlin Harris

It’s a really easy thing to have and I always have access to it. That makes getting my art supplies easier, especially when I have a last-minute project I need to buy something for.
-MIRANDA PEREZ

Griffy’s Art Supply is navigating what it means to be a locally-owned art store in a college town.

For a long time, a convenient resource for Bloomington artists was Pygmalion's Art Supply. The store was around for almost 50 years and became a fixture in the community. However, it permanently shut its doors in June of 2020.

Not even a year after Pygmalion’s close, AJ O’Reilly decided that Bloomington wasn’t Bloomington without an art store. Griffy’s Art Supply began as an online art store but became a tangible location in March of 2021. Located off of West Kirkwood Avenue, the store is a quick walk from Bloomington’s town square.

When coming up with the concept of Griffy’s, O’Reilly says he wanted it to be a business that is rooted in the Bloomington community just like Pyg’s was.

“Coming up with the name of Griffy’s, we wanted it to feel like a Bloomington staple and had been here for a long time, says AJ O’Reilly, store owner. “We don’t want it to feel like a big box store. We want it to feel like a mom-and-pop shop, which it is.”

I think that Bloomington isn’t Bloomington without an art store. So Griffy’s is filling in that gap that Pygmalion’s left when they closed.
-KELLER MAKEMSON

Currently, Griffy’s is the only locally owned art supply store in Bloomington and is a huge resource for IU art students. They have even teamed up with art professors to create specific supply kits for different art classes offered on campus.

“It’s a really easy thing to have and I always have access to it", says Miranda Perez, IU Studio Art Major. "That makes getting my art supplies easier, especially when I have a last-minute project I need to buy something for.”

Art is a major part of Bloomington’s community and can be seen in various spots around the city. Pygmalion’s was a key supplier for many local artists and Griffy’s is a current resource that is bringing that artistry back to Bloomington.

"Just bringing creativity back to the community that we kind of lost", says general manager, Keller Makemson. "I think that Bloomington isn’t Bloomington without an art store. So Griffy’s is filling in that gap that Pygmalion’s left when they closed."
